Tae D. Johnson — Former ICE Acting Director, Now Sabot Consulting

revolving

Government Service

Johnson served 31 years at ICE/INS, rising to Acting ICE Director (January 2021-July 2023). Prior roles:

  • Deputy Executive Associate Director for ERO — oversaw $4.4B budget and 7,900 employees
  • Assistant Director for ERO Custody Management — managed ~40,000 daily detainees across ~250 facilities

GAO found his service after November 16, 2021 violated the Federal Vacancies Reform Act.

Private Sector Role

Johnson is now on the “Criminal Justice team” at Sabot Consulting, the firm that advises county sheriffs on building and operating ICE detention facilities through IGSAs. Sabot describes itself as the county’s “ICE-facing arm” and facilitates “County-ICE-operator alignment.”

The Legal Question

Under 18 U.S.C. § 207, Johnson faces:

  • A lifetime ban on matters he “participated personally and substantially” in as Acting ICE Director
  • The one-year cooling-off period ended July 2024

The question is whether Sabot’s work — facilitating IGSA negotiations between counties and ICE, managing the “federal interface,” coordinating with ICE on inspections and compliance — constitutes the kind of communication with ICE that triggers the lifetime ban. When the former Acting Director of ICE is selling ICE detention services through county intermediaries, the intermediary structure may not provide sufficient legal distance.

Significance

Johnson is the most senior ICE official to move directly into the private detention-consulting space. His presence at Sabot signals to sheriffs that the firm has genuine insider knowledge and ICE relationships — which is exactly the point, and exactly the concern.
