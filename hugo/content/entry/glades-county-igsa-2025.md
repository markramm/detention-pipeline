ICE reinstated the Intergovernmental Service Agreement with [glades-county-fl-detention](/entry/glades-county-fl-detention/) on April 3, 2025, for up to 500 beds. The facility is operated by the Glades County Sheriff's Office. [sabot-consulting](/entry/sabot-consulting/) featured Glades County as a "Client Profile" in their newsletter, indicating Sabot's involvement in the IGSA relationship.

This is one of the existing Florida sheriff-operated IGSA facilities (alongside [baker-county-fl-detention](/entry/baker-county-fl-detention/)) that the Bradford County proposal explicitly cites as models: "Other counties in Florida — including Baker County and Glades County — whose sheriff's offices already operate ICE detention facilities in partnership with the federal government."
