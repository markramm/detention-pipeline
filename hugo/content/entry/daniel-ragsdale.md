# Daniel H. Ragsdale

Daniel H. Ragsdale is an American lawyer and law enforcement official who transitioned from the second-highest position at ICE directly to a senior executive role at [[geo-group|GEO Group]], the agency's largest contractor.

## Career Timeline

1. **ICE Deputy Director (May 2012 - May 2017)** -- Second-in-command at U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement
2. **Acting ICE Director (January 20-30, 2017)** -- Briefly became acting director following Trump's inauguration, replaced by Tom Homan after 10 days
3. **Resigned from ICE (May 2017)** -- Left government service
4. **[[geo-group|GEO Group]] (July 2017 - present)** -- Joined as Executive Vice President for Contract Compliance. Current title: Senior Vice President, Contract Administration and Compliance (effective January 1, 2025)

## Significance

Ragsdale's 2017 departure was an early, high-profile example of the ICE-to-[[geo-group|GEO]] revolving door that drew significant media attention and criticism from civil rights organizations. As ICE's second-ranking official, he had intimate knowledge of the agency's contracting processes, detention standards, facility evaluations, and expansion plans -- all directly relevant to [[geo-group|GEO Group]]'s business.
