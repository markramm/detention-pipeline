## Overview

In May 2025, the Department of Homeland Security conducted an I-9 employment audit at Drumgoon Dairy, a 6,500-cow dairy operation near Lake Norden, South Dakota. The audit resulted in the immediate termination of **38 employees** whose documentation was "inaccurate, outdated or incomplete." The dairy's workforce was reduced from 50+ to just 16 employees — a loss of more than half its workers.

## Why This Matters

The farm is located **5 miles from DHS Secretary Kristi Noem's eastern South Dakota home**, making this a politically charged case that illustrates how federal enforcement priorities impact rural communities that rely on immigrant labor. The owners had never been audited before in nearly two decades of operation.

## Key Facts

- **Facility:** Drumgoon Dairy, Lake Norden, Hamlin County, SD
- **Audit date:** May 2025
- **Conducting agency:** Department of Homeland Security
- **Workers terminated:** 38 (out of ~54 total employees)
- **Workers remaining:** 16
- **Operation:** 6,500-cow dairy, opened 2006 via EB-5 investment program
- **Owners:** Dorothy and Ciaran Elliott

## Impact

- Remaining employees working 13-14 day stretches with only 1-2 days off
- Workplace accidents increased (payloader reversed into manure pond)
- New hires lack years of institutional knowledge lost
- Owners stated: "We didn't just lose employees — we lost years of experience, skills, knowledge and a team culture that we had built over the past 10 years or more."
- SD dairy industry leaders have warned of difficulty finding workers "if we don't allow more laborers in"

## Context

This audit represents the "silent raid" model — no dramatic ICE operation, but a paperwork-based enforcement action that devastates agricultural operations. South Dakota's meat processing and dairy industries employ 58% immigrant workers. The audit came during the same period as Operation Prairie Thunder and broader Trump administration immigration enforcement escalation.
