## Overview

The **Imperial Regional Detention Facility** in Calexico, CA is operated by **Management and Training Corporation (MTC)**, a for-profit private prison company. Located in Imperial County — the **highest-scoring county in the California heatmap (score 100, 4 signal types)** — it sits directly on the US-Mexico border and serves as a primary intake point for border apprehensions.

## Key Details

- **Location**: 1572 Gateway Rd, Calexico, CA
- **Operator**: MTC (for-profit)
- **Detainees**: Adult men, women, asylum seekers, criminal detainees, undocumented migrants
- **Heatmap signals**: 8 IGSAs, 3 ANC contracts, 1 facility, budget distress

## Deaths in Custody

- **Luis Beltran Yanez Cruz**, 68, from Honduras, 26 years in the US — died January 6, 2026. ICE missed the legally required deadline to release its report on the death. ([KPBS](https://www.kpbs.org/news/border-immigration/2026/01/05/ice-misses-deadline-to-release-report-on-in-custody-death-in-imperial-county))
- **Xie** (first name) — died after seizure at facility and transport to nearby hospital ([KPBS](https://www.kpbs.org/news/border-immigration/2026/01/16/these-spaces-are-often-stressful-questions-raised-after-another-death-in-imperial-county))

## Why Imperial County Scores 100

Imperial County has the highest heatmap score in California (and one of the highest nationally) because of the convergence of:
1. **8 IGSAs** — inter-governmental service agreements with ICE
2. **3 ANC contracts** — alternative-to-custody contracts
3. **1 private detention facility** (this one)
4. **Budget distress** — small, poor county dependent on federal detention dollars
5. **Border location** — primary intake for Calexico/El Centro sector

## Oversight Gaps

California gave Imperial County health department authority to inspect the facility, but the department stated it would only respond to a complaint "if the facility falls within our legal authority to inspect" — suggesting limited actual oversight capacity. ([CalMatters, Oct 2025](https://calmatters.org/justice/2025/10/ice-detention-center-inspections/))
