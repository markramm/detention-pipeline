## Overview

The **Tallahatchie County Correctional Facility** is a 2,500-bed maximum-security private prison in **Tutwiler, Mississippi** (Tallahatchie County, Mississippi Delta), operated by **CoreCivic**. The facility holds a mix of populations: Tallahatchie County inmates, Mississippi state inmates, ICE detainees, and U.S. Marshals Service holds. In February 2025, CoreCivic expanded its ICE contract to designate **252 beds** specifically for ICE detainees.

## Key Details

- **Capacity**: 2,500 total beds; 252 designated for ICE
- **Operator**: CoreCivic
- **Location**: Tutwiler, Mississippi (North Delta)
- **Multi-jurisdictional**: Holds county, state, ICE, and USMS populations
- **ICE expansion**: Announced February 27, 2025
- **Part of broader CoreCivic ICE expansion**: Contract modifications covered facilities in MS, NV, OH, and OK collectively adding 784 ICE beds

## Conditions Concerns

- Mississippi Today reported on deaths at the facility in November 2025 ("Men died in a private Delta prison. Who's accountable?")
- Local resident Ted Martin expressed concerns about detainees and requested humane conditions
- CoreCivic's warden and Public Affairs Office did not respond to media inquiries about the ICE expansion

## Context

This is CoreCivic's **second** ICE facility in Mississippi, alongside the much larger Adams County Correctional Center. The expansion follows a national trend of private prison operators repurposing existing capacity for ICE detainees as the Trump administration pushes to expand detention.
