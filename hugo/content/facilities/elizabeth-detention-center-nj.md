## Overview

The **Elizabeth Contract Detention Facility** in Elizabeth, NJ is a **300-bed** CoreCivic-operated ICE detention center, operational since 1997. It is the older of New Jersey's two active ICE detention facilities (the other being the 1,000-bed Delaney Hall in Newark).

## Key Developments (2025-2026)

### State Ban Struck Down (July 2025)

In 2021, Governor Phil Murphy signed a law banning public and private entities from entering contracts for immigration detention. CoreCivic sued. In **July 2025**, the Third Circuit Court of Appeals ruled **2-1** that the NJ law was unconstitutional because it regulates the federal government. This was a major win for CoreCivic and preserved the Elizabeth facility's operations.

### Contract Extension (March 2026)

ICE is planning a **contract extension** for the Elizabeth detention center, per NJ Spotlight News reporting in March 2026.

### Conditions Complaints

Activists have protested conditions at Elizabeth including:
- Undrinkable water
- Spoiled food
- Overcrowding
- Lack of medical care
- Limited legal access

In March 2025, activists protested conditions at Elizabeth even as ICE was announcing the new Delaney Hall facility in Newark.

## Why This Facility Matters

Elizabeth is the anchor facility in NJ's detention infrastructure — nearly 30 years of continuous operation. The Third Circuit ruling striking down NJ's ban on detention contracts has national implications: if states cannot legally block private ICE detention contracts, the sanctuary state strategy has a constitutional ceiling. The contract extension signals that Elizabeth will continue operating alongside the much larger Delaney Hall, giving ICE 1,300+ beds in the Newark/Elizabeth metro area alone.
