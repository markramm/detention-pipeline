# Ste. Genevieve County Detention Center — Ste. Genevieve, MO

## Overview

The Ste. Genevieve County Detention Center, about an hour south of St. Louis in southeast Missouri, has become a major regional ICE holding facility. The sheriff expanded the jail to **500 beds**, with **400 reserved for ICE detainees** under an Intergovernmental Service Agreement (IGSA) at **$103/day** — roughly $15M/year at capacity. It houses ICE detainees transferred from across Missouri (including St. Louis) and southern Illinois. As of late May 2026, around 150 immigrants were held there.

On **May 26, 2026**, U.S. Reps. Wesley Bell (D-MO) and Nikki Budzinski (D-IL) toured the facility as members of the House Oversight Committee and reported serious conditions problems.

### Conditions documented by lawmakers (May 26, 2026)

- **Overcrowding**: A single room held 48 detainees. With beds running out, some sleep on thin inflatable trays a few inches off the floor.
- **Medical neglect**: Pregnant women not receiving prenatal care; detainees not receiving medical care.
- **Water access**: Some detainees said they went so long without drinking water that they resorted to drinking from the shower.
- **Legal/notary access**: Detainees reported difficulty reaching attorneys; reports of inadequate notary access despite jail claims that two notaries are always available.

### Sheriff's Office rebuttal (early June 2026)

After the tour, the Ste. Genevieve County Sheriff's Office issued a detailed public statement (via Sun Times News, early June 2026) disputing the lawmakers' findings point by point:

- **Water**: Every cell and pod has sinks with hot/cold running water plus daily ice coolers; all detainees issued drinking cups with continuous access.
- **Food**: Three hot meals/day (~3,800 calories, ~130g protein); medical/religious diets accommodated.
- **Medical**: On-site staff of three LPNs, a paramedic, an EMT-certified deputy, and a trained CO; an outside healthcare provider contracts with the jail; a physician visits weekly; the hospital is directly across the street.
- **Prenatal**: Pregnancy testing at intake, prenatal vitamins, OB/GYN appointments, extra mattresses, high-protein diets (up to 220g/day), extra snacks, increased monitoring.
- **Overcrowding**: Not directly rebutted; the office said the facility was "specifically engineered and designed" to hold both criminal defendants and civil detainees by federal classification.
- Major Jason Schott called it "a very good meeting and tour" and said the office believes it operates "an excellent detention center."

The rebuttal sets up a contested-conditions standoff: lawmakers' detainee interviews vs. the sheriff's facility-capacity description. Attorney/notary access was not addressed in the rebuttal.

On **June 2, 2026**, KFVS aired a follow-up on a mother and daughter held at the facility awaiting an asylum hearing — continued local coverage keeping the conditions story alive.

## Why This Matters

1. **Second major MO regional ICE hub** alongside Greene County (Springfield). Ste. Genevieve receives transfers from a wide catchment including St. Louis city/county, which themselves refuse 287(g) and now ban local detention — so detainees are exported to rural lockups. See [[missouri-ozarks-ice-corridor]].
2. **Congressional oversight escalation**: The Bell/Budzinski tour is the first sustained federal scrutiny of a Missouri ICE jail's conditions, documenting overcrowding and denied medical care.
3. **Conditions risk pattern**: The neglect findings mirror the Phelps County (Rolla) facility, where a detainee died by suicide in April 2025. See [[phelps-county-jail-rolla-mo]].
4. **No exit via habeas going forward**: Detainees here are in the Eighth Circuit, where *Herrera Avila v. Bondi* (Mar 25, 2026) eliminated the bond-hearing right that had freed many Midwest detainees through 2025-early 2026 — so people now face prolonged detention in a facility with contested conditions rather than release on bond. See [[bond-hearing-circuit-split-2026]].
