# Corrections Center of Northwest Ohio (CCNO) — Multi-County Regional Jail

## Overview
The Corrections Center of Northwest Ohio (CCNO) in Stryker became Ohio's third ICE detention facility in March 2025. It is a multi-county regional jail governed by a board of commissioners and sheriffs from Lucas, Fulton, Williams, Henry, and Defiance counties. The board approved ICE housing in an 8-2 vote, with notable dissent from Lucas County (Toledo metro).

## Key Details
- **ICE beds**: Up to 96 designated for ICE detainees
- **ICE per-diem rate**: $117/day per inmate, plus mileage and officer transport costs
- **Total facility capacity**: 638 inmates
- **Board vote**: 8-2 to approve ICE contract (Feb 26, 2025)
- **Opened to ICE**: Week of March 17, 2025; 22 ICE detainees housed in first week (mostly out-of-state)
- **Expected detention duration**: 40-45 days per detainee
- **Notable dissent**: Lucas County Commissioner Peter Gerken voted no: "I don't want to be part of this mass deportation system. It ruins families. It's unjust."
- **History**: Has housed federal inmates since the 1990s
- **287(g)**: The CCNO also holds a 287(g) agreement covering all five member counties
- **Multi-county FIPS note**: Technically in Williams County (FIPS 39171), but the five-county board means Lucas (39095), Fulton (39051), Henry (39069), and Defiance (39039) are all implicated
