# Caroline Detention Facility — Bowling Green, VA

## Overview

The **Caroline Detention Facility (CDF)** near Bowling Green in Caroline County (FIPS 51033) is one of Virginia's two dedicated ICE detention centers, alongside [[farmville-detention-center-va]]. Since 2018 it has operated under contract with ICE to exclusively detain noncitizens. Rated for **336 beds**, it has been **continuously over capacity since November 2025**.

## Key Details

- **Average daily population:** 353 on Nov. 10, 2025; never dropped below 342 in any reporting period through April 2, 2026, and stood at 344 on April 2 (WRIC). FY2026 average reported at 331 by VPM, with 216 detainees marked as having no ICE threat level.
- **No-threat majority:** Like Farmville, the bulk of those detained have no criminal record, contradicting administration "worst of the worst" rhetoric.
- **Oversight:** Congressman Suhas Subramanyam (VA) visited the facility in 2026 for oversight and introduced the **WATCH ICE Act** for detention transparency. The DHS Office of Inspector General has previously conducted an unannounced inspection of CDF.
- **Why it matters:** CDF and Farmville are the only two formal ICE detention centers in Virginia. Persistent over-capacity at a 336-bed facility is a direct signal of the 2025-2026 enforcement surge (nearly 11,000 ICE arrests in VA since the 2025 inauguration) and feeds demand for the new-facility expansion ICE is pursuing across the state.
