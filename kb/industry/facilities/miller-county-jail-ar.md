# Miller County Jail — Texarkana ICE Detention

## Overview

The Miller County Jail in Texarkana, Arkansas holds immigration detainees under an **Intergovernmental Service Agreement (IGSA)** with ICE. The facility operates under the **Task Force Model** for 287(g) enforcement. Located on the Arkansas-Texas border, it is part of the southern Arkansas enforcement corridor.

## Key Details

- **Location**: 2300 East Street, Texarkana, AR 71854
- **Contract**: IGSA with ICE (ICE makes payments for housing federal detainees)
- **287(g) model**: Task Force Model (street-level enforcement authority)
- **ICE detainees**: 21 people wanted by ICE reported in Bowie (TX) and Miller (AR) county jails as of December 8, 2025
- **ICE inspection**: July 31, 2025 (report at ice.gov)
- **Historical**: Facility has long history of ICE detainee housing; TRAC data shows detainee flows dating to at least 2015

## Why It Matters

Miller County sits at the Arkansas-Texas border on I-30, a major interstate corridor. The Task Force Model gives local officers street-level enforcement authority beyond just jail screening, making this a more aggressive enforcement posture than the Jail Enforcement Model used in Benton County.
