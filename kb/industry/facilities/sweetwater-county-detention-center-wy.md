# Sweetwater County Detention Center — I-80 Corridor ICE Hub

> **Update (May 2026):** Sheriff John Grossnickle presented financials showing the ICE contracts will generate **well over $1 million for the county this fiscal year** — already $167,500 in stipends (transport, equipment, IT) plus ~$370,600 in inmate-housing revenue since October. The detention per diem was **renegotiated from $61.57 to $120/day** for adults in 2025. The county runs both a Warrant Service Officer model (2-3 deputies, since 2020) and a Task Force Model (added 2025), and remains one of the few WY facilities certified to hold federal detainees beyond 72 hours.

## Overview

The Sweetwater County Detention Center in Rock Springs is a 205-bed facility that has become a regional ICE detention hub along the Interstate 80 corridor between Denver and Las Vegas. It was the **first Wyoming law enforcement agency** to join the **287(g) Task Force Model**, an enhanced partnership authorizing up to 5 sworn deputies to perform limited federal immigration enforcement duties including investigations and arrests. The facility has been ICE-certified since **2020**.

## Key Details

- **287(g) agreement**: Task Force Model (first in Wyoming)
- **ICE certification**: Since 2020
- **ICE bed capacity**: Expanded from **15 to 30 dedicated beds**
- **Total facility capacity**: 205 beds
- **Projected annual revenue**: Up to **$3 million** from detention operations
- **Regional coverage**: Accepts detainees from Teton, Lincoln, and Sublette counties
- **Staging role**: Serves as intermediary holding facility between local jails and the Aurora ICE Processing Center

## Strategic Location

Sweetwater County's position along I-80 makes it a natural staging point for ICE operations across western Wyoming. The facility funnels detainees from the Teton/Jackson area (where ICE is active weekly) southward toward Aurora. With the only ICE ERO office in the state in Casper and another planned for Gillette, Sweetwater fills a geographic gap in western Wyoming.

## Community Response

A protest at a Rock Springs hotel lodging Border Patrol officers prompted a police response, indicating some local opposition to the expanding enforcement presence. The ACLU of Wyoming has raised concerns about the 287(g) agreements being signed without public comment.

## ICE Expansion Plans

ICE has indicated it wants to build an enforcement office in **Rock Springs**, which would make Sweetwater County a permanent base of operations for western Wyoming enforcement rather than relying on the Casper ERO office.
